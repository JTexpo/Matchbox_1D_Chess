How to use this app
Upon loading the webpage, you'll notice the AI (white) making the first move, and it'll be your turn (black) to respond. In one-dimensional chess:

  • The rook can move left and right until it collides with another piece.
  • The knight can move two spaces left or right, jumping over any piece on the first space.
  • The king can only move back and forth.

Your objective is to avoid getting checkmated by the AI (white). The game concludes when checkmate occurs. Click the 'Reset' button to start a new game.

What is Match Box Algorithm?
The Match Box Algorithm earned its name in 1961 when Donald Michie crafted a mechanical computer using 304 matchboxes to play tic-tac-toe.

The fundamental idea behind Match Box Algorithms is rooted in recognizing certain games with definitive flows, where the player going first can always secure a win or draw.

Think of the game as a maze. Much like a depth-first search, the algorithm explores various game states until it reaches an undesirable state (a loss or draw).

The crucial move leading to this state is then removed from the algorithm's memory. This strategic approach prevents the AI from repeating the same mistake in future moves.
